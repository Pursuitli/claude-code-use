Slide 3 — The stablecoin‑to‑business payment gap

A stablecoin transfer is not the same as a business‑acceptable payment

For real‑world vendors, receiving value is not enough. The payment must pass internal finance, bank review, tax and accounting treatment, and audit scrutiny. The gap is not blockchain settlement speed — it is turning stablecoin‑funded demand into a payment that looks normal, explainable and acceptable in the real‑world economy.

A business‑acceptable payment needs

  • A clear payer and beneficiary
  • A legitimate business purpose
  • Invoice or contract matching
  • Source‑of‑funds comfort
  • Compliant conversion into fiat where needed
  • Accounting and reconciliation records
  • An auditable payment trail for banks, auditors and management

What makes it bankable

Every stage produces an artefact a bank can inspect — KYB file, screening result, conversion record, settlement confirmation, reconciliation match. The workflow runs live today, manual‑assisted; seed proceeds automate it.

Flow policy — allowed vs excluded

Allowed flowsExcluded flows
  • Invoice‑backed B2B operating expenses (cloud, SaaS, AI/API, professional services)

  • Controlled corridor payouts within approved limits
  • Treasury conversion for onboarded enterprise clients

  • Speculative trading or leverage flows
  • Restricted jurisdictions and blocked counterparties
  • Flows without invoice / purpose evidence
  • Prohibited assets, chains and wallet types

Every allowed flow is one a bank compliance officer can re‑tell in one sentence: who paid whom, for what, with what evidence. Codified in STABO's Permitted‑Flow Policy v1.0, in force since launch.

Compounding operating burden — the defensibility

Each layer must exist before the next becomes possible; together they form the staircase a competitor has to climb in sequence (quantified on Slide 19 and Appendix E). As of June 2026, STABO has completed or is progressing 12+ bank, LP and partner due‑diligence processes — each one a unit of operating history a copy cannot buy. The burden that makes this business operationally heavy is the same burden that keeps it defensible.
